About Me

Software Engineer

My interests range from natural language processing to artifical intelligence to whatever my most recent project is. I love everything about computer science and building software. There is so much to learn and while I hate the impossibility of learning it all, I also enjoy the challenge.

I WANT TO FIX ALL THE THINGS

Before computer science I was in fashion design and creative writing. I've always had strong opinions about things that should exist and don't like relying on other people's whims to create them. Software engineering lets me indulge these same self-reliant tendencies.

"Why hasn't anyone built an app that aggregates the available data on hill steepness, curb ramps, and sidewalk closures?"

"Why should we have to navigate a 12 page, complicated spreadsheet to create our characters for this game?"

"Why isn't there a script that will automatically sort people I follow into Twitter lists?"

*Please note: any sentences ending in prepositions are wholly intentional and not due to illiteracy or ignorance but because the “never end a sentence with a preposition” rule springs from early English grammarians trying to emulate Latin grammar. While Latin sentence structure precludes ending a sentence with a preposition, it is not only possible to do so in English but often much simpler than restructuring an entire sentence. Since English has primarily Germanic, not Latin, roots and since this rule is not based on any need arising from English syntax, I consciously choose to ignore it.
